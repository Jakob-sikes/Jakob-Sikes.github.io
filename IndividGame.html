UX Case Study

Nursery Nightmare: First-person horror maze game focused on user-centered design. Goal: make navigation and object interaction clear while maintaining suspense.

1. Problem

  • Players initially felt lost and disoriented in the maze.
  • Lack of visual cues reduced immersion and clarity.
  • Objective: improve spatial understanding and guide player interactions.

2. Research

  • Initial playtest with 10 participants, 5 sessions recorded in detail.
  • Collected data on navigation, interaction, and puzzle engagement (see PDF).
  • Insight: players needed better cues and prompts to interact effectively.

3. Ideation

  • Improved lighting to highlight paths and doors.
  • Created distinct 3D room assets for orientation.
  • Added in-world prompts for interactions (e.g., "Square to collect").
  • Reorganized puzzles for flow and clarity.

4. Prototype

Greybox blockout (original maze)

Updated maze with lighting and asset improvements

Interactive prompts to guide players

5. Testing & Iteration

  • Observed player sessions and iterated on maze layout, prompts, and lighting.
  • Next playtest scheduled for end of May to validate improvements.

6. Outcome

  • Navigation confusion significantly reduced.
  • Players understood interactions more clearly with in-world prompts.
  • Full UX cycle demonstrated: problem → research → ideation → prototype → testing → iteration.
